# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We want AnkiForge AI to be a welcoming, constructive project for learners and contributors of different backgrounds and experience levels. Participation should make the project safer, clearer, and more useful.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful, patient, and specific.
- Critique ideas and code, not people.
- Assume good intent while accepting evidence and correction.
- Protect other people's study material, credentials, identity, and Anki data.
- Give credit, respect licenses, and disclose relevant conflicts of interest.
- Help newcomers understand the workflow and safety boundaries.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, threats, discrimination, sexualized conduct, or personal attacks.
- Publishing another person's private information or credentials.
- Pressuring someone to share an API key, collection, private notes, logs, or account access.
- Deliberately bypassing review, security reporting, or repository safety controls.
- Sustained disruption, spam, or knowingly misleading claims about product capabilities.

## Scope and enforcement

This code applies in repository issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, project spaces, and public representation of the project. Maintainers may edit or remove content, close or lock threads, reject contributions, or temporarily or permanently restrict participation when behavior is unsafe or harmful.

Report conduct concerns privately through the repository's **Security → Report a vulnerability** channel, noting that the report concerns community conduct. If that channel is unavailable, open a minimal issue asking for a private maintainer contact without including sensitive details. Reports will be handled as privately and fairly as practical; retaliation is not tolerated.

## Attribution

This policy is informed by the principles of the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/), adapted to the privacy and data-safety needs of AnkiForge AI.
